I am a PhD student in Human Informatics at the University of Tsukuba and a research fellow at Harvard University. My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, XR, and Human-AI Interaction, with a particular focus on sports training and embodied experiences. I design and study immersive systems that integrate AI-driven feedback, multimodal sensing, and interactive interfaces to support human skill acquisition.

My work explores how computational systems can augment human perception, decision making, and motor learning through embodied interaction. By combining computer vision, large language models, and mixed reality technologies, I develop and evaluate interactive systems that enable personalized and adaptive training experiences. My research has been published at leading HCI venues such as ACM CHI, aiming to bridge technical innovation with real-world human performance.
